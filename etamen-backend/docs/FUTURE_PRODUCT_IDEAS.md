# Future Product Ideas

Date: 2026-05-08  
Sprint: 35 - Egypt-Scale Health Super App Architecture + Product Expansion Blueprint

These ideas are intentionally not implemented in Sprint 35. They are candidates for later roadmap planning after the physical-device pilot gate and production readiness work.

## 1. Family Profiles

Value:
- One account can manage children, elderly parents, or dependents.

Risk:
- More complex consent, guardianship, and privacy rules.

Safety concern:
- Children and elderly health data require strict access controls.

MVP version:
- Add family member profiles and select profile during booking/health logging.

Later version:
- Shared permissions, caregiver access, pediatric growth tracking, and elder care plans.

## 2. Medical Document Vault

Value:
- Users keep prescriptions, lab results, radiology reports, discharge summaries, and medical notes in one place.

Risk:
- High privacy and storage security burden.

Safety concern:
- Documents must not be exposed through public URLs or provider search.

MVP version:
- Private uploads with categories and patient-only access.

Later version:
- Provider sharing with consent, expiry, audit logs, and AI summarization.

## 3. Insurance Integration

Value:
- Users can filter by accepted insurance and reduce booking friction.

Risk:
- Insurance data changes frequently and needs verification.

Safety concern:
- Do not imply coverage is guaranteed without insurer/provider confirmation.

MVP version:
- Provider-level accepted insurance tags.

Later version:
- Claim support, eligibility checks, pre-authorization, and insurer integrations.

## 4. Emergency Mode

Value:
- Users can quickly find nearby emergency hospitals, ambulance numbers, and red-flag guidance.

Risk:
- Incorrect emergency guidance can cause harm.

Safety concern:
- Must avoid AI diagnosis and push users to emergency services for red flags.

MVP version:
- Static emergency numbers and nearest emergency-enabled provider list.

Later version:
- Location-based emergency routing and verified emergency facility status.

## 5. Home Healthcare

Value:
- Home nursing, injections, physiotherapy, elder care, wound dressing.

Risk:
- Provider credentialing, scheduling, and quality control are harder than clinic visits.

Safety concern:
- Staff identity, scope of service, and patient safety must be verified.

MVP version:
- Home visit service catalog and request workflow.

Later version:
- Staff dispatch, GPS tracking, care notes, and recurring plans.

## 6. Physiotherapy Centers

Value:
- Rehabilitation sessions, packages, and follow-up plans.

Risk:
- Treatment plans need professional oversight.

Safety concern:
- Avoid unsafe exercise advice without provider review.

MVP version:
- Physiotherapy provider profiles and session booking.

Later version:
- Rehab programs, progress logs, and provider-supervised exercises.

## 7. Mental Health

Value:
- Psychologists/psychiatrists and safe support pathways.

Risk:
- Crisis escalation and confidentiality requirements are sensitive.

Safety concern:
- Self-harm or crisis content must escalate clearly and safely.

MVP version:
- Mental health provider discovery and booking with careful copy.

Later version:
- Crisis workflows, secure notes, therapy plans, and consented follow-up.

## 8. Women Health

Value:
- OB/GYN follow-up, pregnancy tracking, cycle-related health content.

Risk:
- Sensitive personal data.

Safety concern:
- Avoid diagnosis and ensure privacy for reproductive health data.

MVP version:
- OB/GYN discovery and pregnancy appointment reminders.

Later version:
- Pregnancy follow-up plans, safe content library, and family sharing options.

## 9. Child Health

Value:
- Pediatric bookings, vaccination reminders, growth tracking.

Risk:
- Requires guardian consent and accurate vaccination schedules.

Safety concern:
- Pediatric guidance must be conservative and professionally reviewed.

MVP version:
- Child family profiles and vaccination reminder schedule.

Later version:
- Growth charts, pediatric care plans, school medical records.

## 10. Chronic Disease Programs

Value:
- Better tracking for diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, obesity, and kidney disease.

Risk:
- Can become medical advice if not bounded carefully.

Safety concern:
- Must escalate dangerous readings and avoid medication changes without a clinician.

MVP version:
- Vitals trends, reminders, and red-flag disclaimers.

Later version:
- Provider-supervised programs and care team dashboards.

## 11. Corporate Wellness

Value:
- Companies can offer health packages for employees.

Risk:
- Employer privacy boundaries and reporting limits.

Safety concern:
- Employers must not see individual medical data without explicit consent and legal basis.

MVP version:
- Company packages and anonymous aggregate reporting.

Later version:
- Employee wellness plans, gym/lab/doctor bundles, HR integrations.

## 12. Provider SaaS Dashboards

Value:
- Providers manage appointments, wallet/settlements, reports, CRM, inventory, lab results.

Risk:
- Large operational surface.

Safety concern:
- Role permissions and audit logs are mandatory.

MVP version:
- Improve current admin/provider panels for appointments, payments, and profile updates.

Later version:
- Provider mobile app, CRM, inventory, reporting, and staff permissions.

## 13. AI Document Summarization

Value:
- Summarize lab/radiology reports in safer plain language.

Risk:
- Users may treat summaries as diagnosis.

Safety concern:
- Must include disclaimers, red-flag escalation, and no treatment prescription.

MVP version:
- Summarize uploaded report text with clear non-diagnostic framing.

Later version:
- Provider-reviewed summaries and structured health timeline.

## 14. Smart Recommendations

Value:
- Better discovery based on location, specialty, availability, and preferences.

Risk:
- Recommendations can become medically inappropriate if based on symptoms.

Safety concern:
- No diagnosis-based routing unless clinically reviewed; use marketplace metadata first.

MVP version:
- Recommend nearby available providers for a selected specialty.

Later version:
- Personalized ranking with transparent controls and opt-out.

## 15. Loyalty / Subscriptions

Value:
- Family subscription, discounted checkup bundles, gym/coach bundles.

Risk:
- Payment, refunds, and benefit accounting complexity.

Safety concern:
- Do not encourage unnecessary medical usage.

MVP version:
- Simple discount bundles for checkups or family plans.

Later version:
- Wallet credits, tiered loyalty, corporate packages, and cross-vertical bundles.
